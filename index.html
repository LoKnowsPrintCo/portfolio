About

I work at the intersection of psychology, AI, product, and education. The counselor training underneath all of it is the engine. I take things people experience as noise, neuroscience, personality, ADHD, the resistance inside an organization, and turn them into something they can actually hold and use.

Community is where I do that in public, and it's the work I want to do next. I care about the same question there that I ask everywhere else: what does this person need to understand before they can move forward?

02

How I build

Understanding before optimization

Technology doesn't fix confusion. Clarity does. I orient a person before I try to speed them up.

Build for the person, not the average user

Every product starts from one real human pattern, not a demographic. Specific beats general.

Make complex ideas feel obvious

If someone leaves smarter instead of overwhelmed, the design worked. That's the whole test.

AI should increase agency, not dependence

Every tool should leave the person more capable than it found them, not more reliant on me.

AI Readiness Scale

Turned an instinct-based sales call into a scored, teachable decision.

ProblemEnrollment specialists can't tell which product to offer a prospect, because there's no shared language for AI readiness, so they pitch on instinct and miss the timing.
IdeaWhat if you could score any prospect across five psychological dimensions in real time and let the data name the right conversation?
ResultAn interactive scoring tool that maps a business owner to a stage on the AI adoption continuum and surfaces the right product, before pricing comes up.
LearnedNaming someone's stage moves a conversation more than any pitch. Timing beats persuasion.

Grounded

A gentle, body-based stress relief experience you move through at your own pace.

ProblemWhen someone is activated, generic calm-down advice asks them to think their way out of a body state. Wrong tool, wrong moment.
IdeaWhat if regulation were paced by the body instead of the clock, something a person could move through at their own speed and return to anytime?
ResultAn interactive, body-based experience that walks someone back toward calm at their own pace, with the option to save or print what worked.
LearnedPacing is the intervention. The moment the tool stopped rushing people, it started working.
